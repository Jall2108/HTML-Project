Who are we?

Panda Relief is a Irish-based international relief and conservation charity established in 2017.

The goal of Panda Relief is to prevent the destruction of panda forests and to reintroduce them across eastern Asia once their numbers have grown.

To learn more, we encourage you to visit our About Us page.


Why pandas?

Pandas are very important to the ecosystem as they help to keep mountain forests healthy by spreading seeds in their droppings.

Pandas also suffer from a loss of habitat due to mass deforestation across eastern Asia. Climate change is also negatively affecting their bamboo supplies.


What's new?

November 2020

Panda populations have seen an unprecedented rise of 1,400 births this year in captivity alone.

Protesters from Panda Relief along the Chiang River have prevented further deforestation in wild panda regions in order to prevent further wildlife losses.

How can you help?

Panda Relief is always looking for donations to help save the panda species.

Organised coordinated protests, safeguarding pandas from poachers, and giving care to pandas in both captivity and the wild are our top priorities.

However, these activities are expensive, and we can't do it alone. Not without your help.
